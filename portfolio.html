About Me

I'm a psychologist specializing in Cognitive Science and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) research, with a deep belief that great design begins with a thorough understanding of users' needs and behaviours.

My work spans academic research and commercial practice — from qualitative interviews and usability testing to high-fidelity prototyping — always grounded in data and centred on people.

Current areas of focus: affective computing, cognitive load in interaction, and AI-assisted design workflows — working to make complex systems feel intuitive for every user.
